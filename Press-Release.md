# What Really Wins NFL Games? Data Reveals the Factors Behind the Final Score

## Every Sunday millions of fans think they know what will happen — this project finds out if the data agrees

## Problem Statement
Each week of the NFL season, oddsmakers set a point spread predicting the margin of victory for every game. These spreads represent the
collective wisdom of professional analysts and billions of dollars of betting market activity. Yet despite this sophistication, upsets happen constantly and the factors that truly drive game outcomes remain poorly understood by the general public. Rest advantages, weather conditions, home field effects, and divisional familiarity all plausibly affect outcomes, but by how much? This project analyzes 10 seasons of NFL regular season games (2014-2024) to identify which measurable factors are most strongly associated with margin of victory.

## Solution Description
Using a dataset of over 2,700 NFL games enriched with Vegas lines, weather data, rest days, and stadium information, this project builds
a statistical model to identify the key drivers of game outcomes. Rather than simply predicting winners, the model focuses on margin of victory, the same metric the betting market uses, and evaluates whether observable game factors can add predictive value beyond what
Vegas already knows. The result is a clear, interpretable ranking of which factors matter most, from home field advantage and rest
differences to wind speed and divisional rivalry effects.
